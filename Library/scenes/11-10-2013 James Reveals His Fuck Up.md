James Reveals His Fuck Up

\"Want a bite?\"

Rachel was eying his burger with a look of longing. \"Why didn\'t you order one if you\'re hungry?\"

\"It\'s not that easy to admit if you want something."

Wasn\'t that the truth. James wanted so much, in the middle of the night to not wake up to horrible screaming. The sight of blood.

He cut his burger in half and slid it over on the french fry plate. Then he ate his in about three bites while she was elegantly cutting hers into bite-size bits that she speared with his fork and ate one at a time. It was so controlled, almost an elegant performance, so much in contrast to her graceless lunge and ball drop on the bowling lane. Same story on the ski slope where she careened and slid on her butt, one foot dragging the snowboard like an anchor. She was tall and gangly, built like him, he realized, but not quite in command of her limbs. He remembered feeling the same way when he was 12, the year he shot up 5\" and felt like his body had gotten away from him.

\"You,\" he said. It was true. It was hard to not look at her and want to hold her hand.

> \"So tell me why you\'re a fuck up.\"

The terror and helplessness of holding her kicking and jerking legs was in his thoughts. How he wanted to help her. To stop the involuntary and embarrassing loss of control with her eyes rolled up in her head and her feet drumming the floor, despite James\' efforts to keep her still.

\"I killed a girl,\" he muttered.

\"How?\"

\"I was driving. We were drunk and stoned. I went off the road in Topanga Canyon and we rolled.\"

\"Is that how you hurt your shoulder?\"

He nodded, feeling the ghost of the ache that was like a reminder that he couldn\'t ignore.

\"She died in the crash?\"

Why did it feel like he was flaying himself alive? His moves felt raw and exposed, yet he sat forward and plowed on. She\'d exposed herself to him in all her ugliness and most vulnerable. It only seemed fair.

\"Worse than that,\" he said. \"I woke up and got out of the car and left her to walk home. She bled to death.\"

\"Did you know she was hurt?\"

\"Oh yeah,\" he said. \"There was blood all over.\" Who would have thought so much blood in her? Macbeth and the shame those words inspired in him the first time he read them.

\"Was she close to you?\"

\"Right next to me in the passenger seat of the Corvette that flew apart like a cardboard car in the crash.\" He shook his head. \"She asked me for a ride home. I didn\'t even know her name until later.\" Later, meaning when the cop who interviewed him when he was being charged with manslaughter and reckless driving, told him it was Charlotte Shantz and he thought it was just his luck to kill a girl named Chance.

He realized belatedly that Rachel had reached across the table. She held one hand with both of her hands, covering the top and the bottom with long fingers like a protective shell. \"I know,\" she said. \"I know.\"

\"Hurts,\" he said.

\"I know,\" she said.

It had been months and months of therapy sessions and Group Groping and calls with his bewildered and frightened parents and he\'d never been able to talk about that night. The blood he\'d wiped on the cheat grass to get it off his hands. The way the girl \-- finally he was able to name her Charlotte \-- had been breathing high in her chest. He\'d tried to rouse her but she\'d been so far under in her unconscious dream land all she would do is groan. He\'d had vertigo and it seemed like it took forever to climb the rock strewn slope the car had tumbled over. Big chunks of fiberglass and what looked like brown paper backing dotted the hillside. A door panel. Part of the hood. A piece from the trunk with a chrome fender nearby. It looked like the car had exploded and unbeknownst to him, he found out what he thought was steel and metal was nothing but flimsy paper.

No wonder there was so much blood. When he walked away from the girl still strapped in her seat by the seatbelt there was almost no car around her. Just chassis and tires and a dashboard.

He didn\'t remember getting home, that some good Samaritan given him a ride and dropped him at the security gate at his parent\'s house. He couldn\'t remember the code and had to lean on the buzzer and tell Lupe the maid to come. his mother drove down the driveway and took one look at him and drove him straight to the hospital. After that it was all a blur.

Now he knew that the girl had been breathing her last with a \_\_\_\_\_. And wind ruffling her henna-red hair. Alone while James was being ferried to the emergency room like a prince.

The thought of what he had done just about cracked his chest open. He looked at her catsup on the plate and wondered if the sight of red would always make him want to lay his head down and howl.

He didn\'t want forgiveness. He wasn\'t expecting a benediction or blessing or relief from his guilt. It wasn\'t being fair. She\'d revealed her own self at its ugliest, not that she had any control over her epilepsy or how it commandeered her body and threw it into an alien nobody recognized. James felt like a kindred spirit. His body \-- the one he was so used to depending on for his balletic leaps at the basket and his swoopy throws \-- his body had betrayed him at the crucial moment. It had walked him, hurt and limping, while he held his forearm against his middle so it didn\'t bring him to his knees with pain, up the side of mesquite strewn slope away from someone he should have saved.

Jesus. How Rachel could even look at him in his naked ugliness, he didn\'t understand.
