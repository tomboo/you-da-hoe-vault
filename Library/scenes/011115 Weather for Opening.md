011115 Weather for Opening

A curtain of falling snow cut off the western view of a meadow that stretched out to blue foothills.

The curtain of streaming snow was so thick it cut off any hint of the blue-black wall of fir trees that ringed the yard. The sun hung out there somewhere. Too weak and wan to do more than ooze sulfurous yellow light through the swirling flakes.

The snow cut off any hint of the giant blue-black fir trees that ringed the yard in a thick, impenetrable wall. The sun was too wan to break through the dimness. Sulfurous yellow light oozed through the curtain so you knew the sun was out there somewhere but it was too weak to fight off the gloom.
