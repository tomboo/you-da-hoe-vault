052014 Rachel and wilderness observation

In Idaho you got the sense of both horizontal and vertical distance. Wasn't it supposed to be Montana that was called Big Sky Country? Maybe they named Montana back in the days of territories and trappers and Indian shit. Before Idaho became a state? In Chicago where she grew up, Rachel's view of the world was horizontal. Houses and humanity stretched as far as the eye could see. Here, there was nothing that moved. Only white

Close in on the trail, Ponderosa pine press in on them. The boughs overhead are laden with snow and every few minutes, a clump of snow loosens and whumps down. It was like their footfalls would trigger gravity and you felt unsure when gravity would descend on you.

She was used to the cold so it didn't bother her.
