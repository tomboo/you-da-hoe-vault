May Sarton writes novels so she can answer a question.

What question are you trying to answer by writing this novel?

My question is how do new young people on the cusp of maturity make their life stories that move then into functioning adults. It\'s a time of confusion. My characters all have strikes against them \-- bulimia, sex addition, drugs, \_\_\_\_ \_\_\_\_\_, suicidal \_\_\_, major depression \-- and they all have to decide how to live.

I\'m questioning how people get up in the morning and live with themselves after horrible behavior. I want to know how it is that some people grow by dribs and spurts and others blast through and some remain stuck in stasis. They all have to decide how to live.

What makes a great novel?

I like a good story where things change from beginning through middle to the end.

I like funny tragic characters in life-and-death situations who approach the \_\_\_\_\_ with a mordant sense of humor.

I like an interplay between nature and my characters. Nature is always in the background making commentary on the action.

I like novels that have an archetypal underpinning. Not sure what mine could be for NaNo.

I like novels that include a love story.

I llike novels that are willing to piss and shit and misbehave \-- no preciosity.

What does the character want?

old Girl wants to escape life in any way possible.

I wonder if I can cut up \_\_\_\_\_ and Old Girl\'s scenes into small parts and treat from them the story. Structural issue of having to pulp fiction cliff hanger at beginning that circles around to the same scene at the end.

In Atkinson novels, Jackson Brodie is the main character, both hero and anti-hero. Characters pivot around him.

Could I work out Old Girl\'s story so that she is the heroine and the pivot point like Jackson?

What prevents Old Girl from killing herself?

The rules and regs of the treatment program keep Old Girl from killing herself. People rat on her. people save her. She is young and inept and fucks up her attempts at suicide. Her aunt and uncle make her feel guilty and tether her to this world.

What is Old Girl\'s epiphany? When Old Girl runs out to the garbageman\'s truck and wrenches open the door, she realizes in a nano-second that the man with the steering column in his chest is really dying. He doesn\'t want to die. He says, \"Help me. Stay with me.\" And she holds his hand as he bleeds to death.
