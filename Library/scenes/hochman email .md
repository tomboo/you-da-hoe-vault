Dear Gail,

 

 

Fifteen years ago, when *Poets & Writers* profiled you as Bob Shacochis' best and most loyal advocate, I told myself I wanted to work with you. It\'s taken me all this time to learn to tell a compelling story.

 

My 70,000 novel, *You-Da-Ho* is about four young adults who are locked up together at Inner Horizons, a psychiatric treatment program in rural Idaho. Treatment is supposed to transform them from crazy to sane but nobody seems to realize that the doctor who runs the program is writing prescriptions for people who aren't there while a real psychopath stalks the treatment center in a garbage truck. 

 

My novel asks the reader to determine the thin line between normal adolescent craziness and serious mental illness through the voices of snarky and profane young people who have experienced extreme tragedy and loss. At the emotional center of the story is a lonely, parentless 18-year-old called Old Girl who has to decide between the bliss of suicide and the pain of life.

 

**Bio**

 

I\'m a literary fiction writer and experienced marketer. My short stories have appeared in *Zoetrope: All Story*, *Writer's Digest*, Humboldt State University\'s *Toyon*, the literary magazine of Manhattanville College of New York, and others.  I teach social media and blogging at Richard Hugo House, School of Visual Concepts and the University of Washington in Seattle.  My Twitter handle is \@twopens2.

 

May I send you the first fifty pages of You-Da-Ho?

 

 

Cynthia Hartwig

 

 

PS I work as hard as Bob but I\'m not as cranky.
